Narrative

Maya woke up to something she didn't expect when she first signed up for PersonalOS: a push notification with money attached. While she slept, the platform's scheduled Harvest pulled her bank Transaktions through her Plaid Konnection, encrypted every record end-to-end, and wrote the ciphertext to Arweave. Her phone then ran Scoring in the background — decrypting and processing on-device only, computing fresh Insight scores without any raw financial data ever leaving her hands. The highest-scoring Insight, automotive.new_vehicle_purchase at 0.87, reflected months of car-related transactions she'd honestly forgotten about.

When the Exchange ran at 3:41 am, it found a live auto dealer Listing bidding $4.50 per Claim — well above Maya's self-set Yield floor of $2.00 for that Signal Type. The Offer was created and queued. By 9:00 am she had a push notification. Two taps and $4.50 USDC landed in her non-custodial Coinbase Smart Wallet on Base. The entire settlement was atomic, on-chain, and required zero trust in PersonalOS — if the escrow didn't release, the Claim didn't register. Later that afternoon she adjusted her Consent floor for travel.frequent_flier upward, quietly filtering out low-value Listings without anyone being notified.

By 6:00 pm she opened the Wallet tab, saw her balance, and initiated a Withdrawal to her bank account via the Coinbase off-ramp. No yield-sharing with a data broker. No behavioral profile sold. She earned because she chose to, on her terms, and she can revoke every Consent at any time with no penalty.

Data Visibility

  • Offer bid amount ($4.50) and Signal Type category
  • Personalized Offer creative from the Brand
  • Their own Insight scores and Ledger Depth
  • Consent settings they configured (floor per Signal Type)
  • Wallet USDC balance and full transaction history
  • Konnection status (established / syncing / disconnected)
  • Harvest schedule and last-run timestamp
  • Withdrawal status and ACH confirmation
Soul never sees
  • Which specific Brand funded the Offer
  • How many other Souls were matched to the same Listing
  • Raw Transaktion ciphertext on Arweave
  • Other Souls' Insight scores or Consent floors
  • PersonalOS internal match-scoring logic
  • Brand's total Listing Budget or Campaign strategy
  • Exchange match rate or platform revenue metrics

Narrative

The campaign manager at an auto dealership network opened the PersonalOS Brand portal at 9:00 am with a clear brief: reach people actively in the market for a new vehicle, not people who clicked a car ad once six months ago. PersonalOS doesn't sell segments — it sells Signal Types. The manager picked automotive.new_vehicle_purchase, set the bid at $4.50, wrote the Offer creative, and set a $500 USDC Budget. The entire setup took eleven minutes. No audience CSV to upload, no lookalike modeling, no pixel to install.

After a brief review queue (Platform Admin approved in 32 minutes), the manager funded the escrow on Base. The moment the USDC confirmed on-chain, the Listing was live and the Exchange ran its first match. By end of day, 47 Souls had voluntarily accepted the Offer — each one had a high Insight score for vehicle purchase intent and had explicitly set a Yield floor at or below $4.50. The Brand paid only for confirmed Claims. No impressions billed, no click fraud, no viewability ambiguity. Each Claim was an on-chain atomic settlement, verifiable on Base.

The dashboard showed 47 Claims, $94 in spend, and $406 remaining in escrow. Critically, the Brand saw zero PII — no names, no transaction data, no location. Just the Signal Type that triggered the match, the timestamp, and the Yield paid. High-intent reach with privacy preserved for the Soul. The manager raised the bid to $5.00 before EOD to win more matches in the next Exchange run.

Narrative

The Platform Admin's morning begins in the review queue — three Listings waiting for judgment. This is the human trust layer of PersonalOS. Two clear approvals: established Brands, Signal Types used correctly, Offer creative factual. The third is a newly onboarded auto parts Brand whose creative promised "guaranteed cash back" with no terms. Rejected with code MISLEADING_OFFER_CONTENT. The Brand was notified within minutes with a detailed rejection reason and an invitation to resubmit with accurate language. PersonalOS's value to Souls depends entirely on Offers being trustworthy — the review queue is not a rubber stamp.

At 10:00 am, a new Signal Type request landed from a Brand wanting to reach homeowners planning major renovations. The Admin validated the proposed scoring schema against what Scoring could reliably derive from bank Transaktions, confirmed internal ML team sign-off, and published home_improvement.major_renovation v1.0 to the catalogue. Within hours, three Brands had expressed interest. At 11:00 am the Exchange health dashboard showed a strong 73% match rate across 12 live Listings with $2,400 in active escrow — one outlier flagged for low Soul Consent adoption in its Signal Type, which signals either poor awareness or low Soul interest in that category.

The afternoon brought a support ticket: a Soul's Plaid Konnection had silently expired, halting Harvests for three days. The Admin traced the Konnection status, triggered a re-auth deep-link, confirmed Harvest resumed, and closed the ticket in 22 minutes. By 4:00 pm the daily revenue picture was healthy: $340 in Claim take-rate, two new Brand subscriptions added via Stripe. The platform is not a data marketplace in the traditional sense — PersonalOS never reads plaintext data and never sells audiences — but the economics are strong precisely because the consent model drives higher Claim conversion than any lookalike audience ever could.

Data Visibility

Admin sees
  • All Listing submissions: Signal Type, bid, creative, Brand identity
  • Konnection status per Soul (not Soul's raw financial data)
  • Harvest run history and error logs (metadata only)
  • Exchange health metrics: match rate, Claim conversion, escrow totals
  • Platform revenue: take-rate per Claim, Brand subscription revenue
  • Signal Type catalogue and version history
  • Support ticket context (error codes, timestamps, Konnection state)
  • Aggregate Claim counts per Listing (no per-Soul identity)
Admin never sees
  • Raw bank Transaktions or email content (always encrypted)
  • Individual Soul Insight scores or Consent floor values
  • Which Soul accepted which Offer (Claim is anonymous)
  • Arweave ciphertext contents — no decryption key held by platform
  • Soul's passkey or Wallet private key
  • Brand bidding strategy or internal campaign goals
